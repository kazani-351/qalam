Qalam [qa-lam] noun A pen for cultivating words. A hand-rebuilt study of a well-made web page, carrying a live rich-text editor you can write in right now.

Try the pen

This is a real editor, not a picture of one. Click anywhere and start writing — make things bold, add headings and lists from the menu, undo your regrets.

Whatever you write stays in your browser and will still be here when you come back. Nothing is sent anywhere.

What this is

  • Schema-Based Editing

    The editor runs Wordgard's full schema — headings, lists, tables, figures — with the document structure enforced, not free-form HTML.

  • Full History

    Undo and redo work the way they should, through the menu or the keyboard shortcuts you already know.

  • Persistent

    Your text is saved to your browser's local storage as you type. No account, no server, no network requests.

  • No Build Step

    Hand-written HTML and CSS, ES modules wired with an import map. View source — that's the whole architecture.

  • Open Source Bones

    The editor is Wordgard, an MIT-licensed rich-text editor library. This page ships its modules unmodified, license included.

  • A Design Study

    The layout, rhythm, and typography here are studied from wordgard.net — rebuilt by hand to learn why they work.

About

Qalam (qalam, قلم — pen) is a design study of wordgard.net, the home of Marijn Haverbeke's Wordgard editor library. That page is one of the quietly best-designed library sites on the web, so I rebuilt it by hand to understand it: same bones, same rhythm, my own words and art.

Nothing was copied. The layout and typographic system were studied and re-implemented; the illustrations on the original are by Kamila Stankiewicz and appear nowhere here — every mark on this page is hand-written SVG. The editor itself is the real Wordgard library (MIT), used with attribution and admiration.

The source of this page is public. A written companion piece — what rebuilding a good design teaches you — is on its way.
